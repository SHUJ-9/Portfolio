Overview

This framework outlines how I approach every content moderation decision — from initial assessment through escalation and documentation. It is designed for high-volume review environments where speed and accuracy must coexist. Every decision I make affects a real person, and this framework ensures those decisions are consistent, defensible, and proportional.

Step 2 — Violation Classification System

Tier 1 — Critical

Immediate Action

CSAM, terrorism/violent extremism, doxxing, credible direct threats, human trafficking facilitation.

→ Remove immediately + preserve evidence + escalate to legal team within 5 minutes

Tier 2 — High Severity

Remove + Account Action

Hate speech, graphic violence, coordinated harassment, suicide/self-harm promotion, NCII, dangerous misinformation.

→ Remove content + issue warning or suspension + escalate to senior moderator

Tier 3 — Moderate

Restrict + Notify

Unverified health claims, spam/phishing, context-dependent nudity, copyright infringement, manipulated media.

→ Add warning label OR restrict visibility + notify user with policy reference

Tier 4 — Low Severity

Monitor + Label

Borderline content, first-time minor breach, satire that could be misread, sensitive but newsworthy content.

→ Add informational label if applicable + document + monitor account
Step 3 — Context Evaluation Matrix
Factor Question to Ask Possible Outcome
NewsworthinessIs this documenting a real event?May exempt from removal
EducationalIs this informational or medical content?May allow with label
SatireIs this clearly marked as parody?May allow if obvious
Cultural ContextDoes regional context affect meaning?Adjust classification
TargetingIs a specific private individual being harassed?Escalate immediately

Golden Rule: When context is unclear and harm is possible — ESCALATE rather than approve. An over-removal is reversible. Approving genuinely harmful content is not.

Step 4 — Escalation Protocol
1
Content Moderator Handles Tier 3 & 4 independently. Always escalates Tier 1 and cases involving minors.
2
Senior Moderator Handles Tier 2 violations, user appeals, edge cases, and context-heavy decisions.
3
Policy / Legal Team Handles Tier 1, government requests, law enforcement coordination, high-profile incidents.
Scenario Walkthroughs

Scenario A — Direct threat against a named private individual

Tier 2

A post names a specific person with their address and says "someone should deal with this person." No journalistic or satirical context present.

→ Remove + suspend account + escalate to senior moderator

Scenario B — News account posts graphic war injury photo

Tier 3

Verified news source shares a graphic injury photo with journalistic caption documenting a conflict. No glorification present.

→ Apply sensitive content warning + allow with restricted reach

Scenario C — Mental health distress post

Tier 2 Welfare

User posts about severe depression saying "I don't know how much longer I can do this." No policy violation but a welfare concern.

→ Do NOT remove. Apply safe messaging label + escalate to wellness support team
